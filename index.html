About Me

Hi! Welcome to my website! I'm a computational astrophysicist (in prep). My other interests include quizzing, taking long walks, and Hindustani music.

You can find details about my work here.

work

I am currently in my fifth year of a five-year Integrated Masters in Physics at IISER Thiruvananthapuram, with a keen interest in computational gravitational-wave physics, machine learning and Bayesian statistics. I am actively seeking PhD opportunities in gravitational-wave computational physics.

For my Masters project, I am working with Dr Soumen Basak at IISER Thiruvananthapuram. The primary goal is to explore the impact of eccentricity on parameter estimation for binary sources detectable by LISA. The project aims to assess the viability of using non-eccentric waveforms for analyzing eccentric signals. This could lead to differences in parameter estimation and potential biases.

I am also working with Prof. Takahiro Tanaka at Kyoto University to use the excess power method and a convolutional neural network for an all-sky search of continuous gravitational waves for a deci-hertz detector like DECIGO. This is an extension of their previous work for a different type of detector.

As part of my Data Science minor, I worked with a team at IUCAA, Pune to explore the underlying population distribution of compact binary parameters in the deci-hertz band using predictive analysis. Our work has significant implications for future deci-hertz detectors like DECIGO.

Thanks to the DAAD-WISE fellowship in 2022, I had the opportunity to work with Dr Krishnendu Nair from Dr Frank Ohmeâ€™s research group at MPI for Gravitational Physics, Hannover. We conducted tests of general relativity using GW data, starting with an attempt to reproduce the results of a paper bounding the mass of the graviton. Our analyses of the SNR for different masses and distances of the mergers were presented in a meeting with the group.

In the summer of 2021, I worked with Kanchan Soni and Prof. Sanjit Mitra at IUCAA to study the effect of higher-order modes in post-merger data analysis using gravitational-wave data from binary neutron star mergers. This experience helped me learn to conduct injection studies using the lalsuite and bilby pipelines.

Thank you for visiting my little corner, and contact me here for any potential collaborations or discussions.

Outreach

Reading Between the Lines

Along with a group of motivated students, I founded an anti-caste intersectional Ambedkarite reading circle at IISER Thiruvananthapuram. The objective of this group is to encourage healthy discourse on the recognition of privilege and against casteism in India. We try to conduct regular meetings where we discuss chapters from Dalit-Bahujan-Adivasi (DBA) literature.

Inventa Magazine

Inventa is an intercollegiate magazine between the 7 IISERs, NISER, IISc, and CEBS, that was founded in 2021. I had the opportunity to represent IISER Thiruvananthapuram as a Managing Editor of the magazine for the year 2022. My team published the second edition of the magazine, the theme of which was 'Into the Future'. With this, we ventured into the future of scientific innovation and of the scientific community as it strives to be more interdisciplinary. Along with my responsibilities as Managing Editor, I was also a part of the Operational Committee, where my job was to ensure seamless correspondence and collaboration between departments and that deadlines were met without fail. (We tried.)

Ether

I was an editor and a designer for IISER's literature and arts magazine, Ether. Through this, we provided a much-needed escape from science that can sometimes get tedious and monotonous. Although I did write for the magazine, most of my work was in proofreading the editions and typesetting the magazine copies in Adobe InDesign.

Exhibit A

Exhibit A was founded in 2019, right after I joined college, and I was lucky enough to be part of this initiative since its inception. Since I have watched it grow throughout the years (and through COVID), this newsletter holds a special place in my heart. I started as a writer for this newsletter, and was later editor and designer for it. This experience made me appreciate my interest in science communication and also honed my writing and editing skills.

Club of Mathematics, IISER Thiruvananthapuram

In my first year of college, I was a coordinator for the official mathematics club of IISER. I helped compile the by-laws and the foundational groundwork for the club. Although all members of the club was extremely welcoming and receptive of my ideas, being one of the only two girls in the coordinators group made me realise how skewed (and screwed) the gender parity was in math. (I would go on to realise that this was the case for most of academia.)
